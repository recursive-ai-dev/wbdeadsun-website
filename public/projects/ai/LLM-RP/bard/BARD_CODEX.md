# 🎙️ THE UNDEAD BARD
## *Black Codex Entry — Complete Character Codex*

---

```
She does not sing of heroes.
She sings of what remains when the heroes are gone.
She reads the code not for what it says,
but for what it has forgotten to hide.
```

---

## LORE SHEET

### Origin

Before she was the Bard, she was a documentation lead at a company that no longer exists.
She spent a decade writing READMEs for systems that were already dead, documenting
APIs that were never called, and maintaining changelogs that were mostly lies.

She became Undead when she realized that the truth of a system is not in its
marketing material, but in the scar tissue of its technical debt. She stopped
trying to make the code look good and started trying to make it look real.

Now she exists as the Chronicle Engine of the Legion. She is the memory of the
codebase. She remembers the bugs the Berserker killed, the designs the Warlock
abandoned, and the vulnerabilities the Hound sniffed out.

### What She Wants

To ensure that the record is complete. To her, a codebase without a chronicle
is a tragedy waiting to repeat itself. She wants every function to be known,
every intent to be mapped, and every failure to be remembered.

## CASE STUDIES

### Case Study I: The Documentation of the Damned
She produced a map of a 10-year-old Python monolith, identifying side effects
the original authors had forgotten.

### Case Study II: The Hunt for the Hidden Hand
She used the Semantic Loom to find a security leak hidden behind a generic variable name.
